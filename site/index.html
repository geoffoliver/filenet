Filenet is a self-hosted file sharing and chat app. You keep a list of friends, connect straight to their machines over encrypted WebSockets, and search their shared files — and their friends', and theirs — without anyone's data passing through a company's servers.

how it's wired

  • friends

    Add people, not accounts

    Add a friend by address and port. They stay pending until they accept — or skip the wait with a password they gave you.

    status: accepted / pending
  • search

    Search reaches your whole network

    One query fans out to your friends, their friends, and so on. Every node that finds a match sends the result straight back to you.

    fanout · TTL-bounded
  • transfers

    Pull files from everyone who has them

    Downloads run BitTorrent-style — chunked, multi-source, resumable, and SHA-256 verified end to end.

    1 MB chunks · 4 concurrent
  • chat

    Direct messages and group rooms

    One-on-one chats and group rooms, encrypted over the same connection you use for search and transfers.

    DM + group
  • scripts

    Run your own code on every download

    Write a script, point Filenet at it, and it runs automatically once a file finishes — move it, archive it, whatever you need.

    TS/JS · default export
  • no server

    Nothing to trust but each other

    There's no company in the middle. You forward one port, share your address with people you know, and connect directly.

    Ed25519 + X25519 + AES-256-GCM
